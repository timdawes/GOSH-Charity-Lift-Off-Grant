# GOSH-Charity-Lift-Off-Grants: Discovery Research
Repository for R Code for Grant Application March 2026


This repository is set up to accompany a grant application to the GOSH Charity Lift Off Grant Scheme (March 2026)
Applicants: Tim Dawes & Pavithra Rajendran

## Title: AI Analysis of Echocardiogram Reports to Improve Paediatric Cardiac Surgery Outcomes

Around 1 in 100 children are born with heart abnormalities, and one-third require open heart surgery using a
heart-lung bypass machine. While lifesaving, this procedure can temporarily weaken the heart, reducing blood and
oxygen supply to vital organs such as the brain, kidneys and liver. This can lead to permanent organ damage,
longer hospital stays and slower recovery.

Right now, doctors find it hard to know which children are most at risk of these complications, meaning treatments
are often started only after damage has occurred. Heart function is regularly checked using ultrasound scans, and
doctors write their findings in free-text reports. These reports are rich in detail, but the format makes it difficult to link scan findings across patients to outcomes and treatments, limiting their use in predicting complications or understanding which treatments help children recover fastest.

## What we plan to do:
This project will analyse ~25,000 anonymised heart scan reports from ~2,500 children treated at GOSH. Using
advanced computer methods we will:
- Extract key measurements from written reports.
- Translate doctors’ descriptions into consistent, comparable scales.
- Group similar patients based on heart structure and function.
- Link these groups to treatments and recovery to learn which treatments work best and for which children.
  
## Who is this research for? How will it help?
This research will enable earlier identification of children at risk of complications and support more
personalised treatment decisions:
- For children and families: more personalised treatments and fewer complications.
- For clinicians: clearer evidence to guide treatment decisions.
- For research: the ability to combine large numbers of scan reports to understand how heart function,
treatments and recovery are connected.

### Overall, this project will help deliver safer surgery with better recovery for children with heart disease.
